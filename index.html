Native american residential schools

thesis statement

When the English colonists opened up the first residential
school, they became responsible for breaching the human
rights of the whole Native American population. The abuse
that occurred there slowly eradicated their right to practice
their culture, religion, speak their language, and essentially
stripped them of their identities. Native peoples’ culture still
hasn’t recovered from years of being destroyed and forgotten,
and it will likely never be the same.

Backround

The establishment of Indian residential schools was an attempt
to assimilate Indigenous children into Euro-centric North American
culture, which began in the early 17th century when the first
pilgrims began to colonize the states and canada. Relations
between Natives and Colonizers were tense, and some attempt
at peace was made in the beginning, but lots of lives were
lost in the total of battles between Indians and Europeans.

Build Up

The Civilization Fund Act, also known as the Indian Civilization Act, was an Act passed by the United States Congress on March 3, 1819. The Act encouraged activities of benevolent societies in providing education for Native Americans and authorized an annuity to stimulate the "civilization process".[1] Thomas L. McKenney lobbied Congress in support of the legislation. It was originally intended to support schools in native villages and areas, such as those established by religious missions.
